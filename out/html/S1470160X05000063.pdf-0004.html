Microbial loss thus increased under elevated C02 where the depletion in inorganic nitrogen seems to be the driving variable of microbial physiological status, in fact the addition of nitrogen lowers the qC (—1.08 and —1.77 in FACE + N and control + N, respectively) (Table 3).

The metabolic quotient is negatively and signiﬁ- cantly affected by FACE and N fertilization treatments (Tables 1 and 4). Face lowers qCOz by 17% in not fertilized and by 23% in fertilized plots while the addition of nitrogen causes a further decrease of this index by 25 and 42% (p < 0.001) in control and FACE plots, respectively (Fig. 1C). In fact qCOz reaches, at the end of 2003, values of 1.86 and 1.43 for control + N and FACE+ N versus 3.86 and 2.90 for control and FACE (Table 1).
